<p><p>The low power chip market is comprised of different types of power consumption. Dynamic power consumption refers to the energy consumed during chip operation and changes with the activity level. Static power consumption, on the other hand, is the energy consumed when the chip is idle or in standby mode. Surge power refers to the sudden increase in power consumption when the chip transitions from idle to active state or vice versa. These various power consumption types are crucial in developing energy-efficient chips to meet the demands of different applications.</p></p>
