<p><p>Polyester artificial marble, also known as engineered marble, is a composite material that mimics the look and feel of natural marble. It is made by mixing aggregates, such as marble dust or limestone, with a polymer resin binder. The resulting material is then molded and polished to achieve a smooth and glossy finish. Polyester artificial marble is widely used in various applications, including countertops, flooring, wall cladding, and bathroom vanities, due to its durability, seamless appearance, and cost-effectiveness compared to natural marble.</p><p>The polyester artificial marble market is experiencing significant growth. <p><strong>The Polyester Artificial Marble Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Pour Forming</li><li>Compression Molding</li><li>Bulk Scrap Molding</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The polyester artificial marble market offers different types of manufacturing processes to produce synthetic marble products. Pour forming involves pouring liquid polyester resin into a mold to create marble slabs or tiles. Compression molding method uses a combination of polyester resin, filler materials, and pigments that are compressed under high pressure in a mold to produce marble products. Bulk scrap molding involves using recycled marble scraps mixed with polyester resin, allowing for the production of sustainable and cost-effective synthetic marble products.</p></p>
