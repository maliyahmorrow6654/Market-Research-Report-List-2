<p><p>Surfactant cleansers are substances used in cleaning and personal care products to reduce the surface tension between the liquid and solid they are applied on. Anionic surfactants have a negatively charged head and are commonly used in shampoos and soaps for their ability to remove dirt and oil. Cationic surfactants, on the other hand, have a positively charged head and are often used as antimicrobial agents and fabric softeners. Both types of surfactants play a vital role in enhancing the cleaning and conditioning properties of many consumer products.</p></p>
