<p><p>Semiconductor Advanced Packaging refers to the technology used to integrate multiple components into a single chip, enhancing efficiency and performance. Fan-Out Wafer-Level Packaging (FO WLP) involves redistributing I/Os to the chip's periphery, increasing chip density and enabling high-speed connections. Fan-In Wafer-Level Packaging (FI WLP) connects multiple chips on a single wafer, reducing costs and enhancing performance. Flip Chip (FC) technology connects the chip directly to the substrate, enhancing electrical performance and thermal dissipation. The 2.5D/3D market involves stacking multiple chips vertically or horizontally, increasing functionality and reducing size.</p></p>
