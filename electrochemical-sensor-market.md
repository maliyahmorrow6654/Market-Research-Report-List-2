<p><p>Electrochemical sensors are commonly used in various industries for detecting and measuring chemical substances. Potentiometric sensors rely on the measurement of electrical potential difference and are ideal for pH and ion concentration detection. Amperometric sensors work by measuring electric current and are commonly used for gas sensing. Conductometric sensors measure changes in electrical conductivity and are suitable for detecting gases and liquids. Each type of sensor has specific applications and advantages, but they all rely on electrochemical reactions to provide accurate and reliable measurements.</p></p>
