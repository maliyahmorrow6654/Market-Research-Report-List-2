<p><p>Power resistors are an essential component in various electronic devices that help regulate and control the flow of electrical current. They are classified into three types based on their mounting style: chassis mounted, surface mounted, and through-hole mounted. Chassis mounted resistors are securely mounted on the device's chassis, providing stability and durability. Surface mounted resistors are mounted directly on the device's circuit board, allowing for space-saving and efficient installation. Through-hole mounted resistors have leads that pass through holes on the circuit board, enabling secure and reliable connections.</p></p>
