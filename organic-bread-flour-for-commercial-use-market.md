<p><p>Organic bread flour for commercial use is available in two market types: machine milled flour and stone ground flour. Machine milled flour is produced using automated processes that involve grinding the grains into a fine powder. It offers consistent and refined quality while maintaining nutritional value. On the other hand, stone ground flour is made by grinding the grains between two stones, preserving the grain's natural properties and resulting in a coarser texture. Both options cater to the commercial baking industry's demand for organic bread flour.</p></p>
