<p><p>Ultra-Wideband (UWB) is a wireless communication technology that uses a very wide frequency spectrum to transmit data over short distances. UWB has two main market types: Impulse Radio and Multi-Band UWB. Impulse Radio UWB utilizes short-duration pulses to transmit data, providing high accuracy and low power consumption, making it suitable for applications like radar systems and precision tracking. Multi-Band UWB, on the other hand, operates by transmitting data over multiple frequency bands simultaneously, offering higher data rates and longer transmission ranges, making it suitable for applications like high-speed wireless data transfer and multimedia streaming.</p></p>
