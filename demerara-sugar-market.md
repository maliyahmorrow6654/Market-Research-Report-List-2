<p><p>Demerara sugar is available in two market types: crystal and syrup. Crystal demerara sugar is obtained by evaporating the juice from Demerara cane until it forms large golden-brown sugar crystals. It has a coarse texture and a slightly sticky nature. On the other hand, Demerara syrup is made by dissolving these crystals in water, resulting in a thick, sticky liquid with a rich caramel-like flavor. Both crystal and syrup demerara sugar are used as sweeteners and flavor enhancers in various food and beverage products.</p></p>
