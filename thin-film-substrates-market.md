<p><p>The thin film substrates market consists of two main types: rigid thin-film substrates and flexible thin-film substrates. Rigid thin-film substrates are sturdy and inflexible, commonly made of materials like glass or ceramics. They are used in applications that require high durability and stability, such as electronic devices and solar panels. On the other hand, flexible thin-film substrates are bendable and can be made of materials like plastic or metal foils. They are highly suitable for applications that demand flexibility, such as wearable electronics and flexible displays.</p></p>
