<p><p>Compact fuel cell market types include Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs), Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC), Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC), and Direct Methanol Fuel Cells (DMFC). PEMFCs use a polymer membrane to separate the electrodes and produce electricity, suitable for portable applications. SOFCs operate at high temperatures and use a solid ceramic electrolyte, making them suitable for stationary power generation. MCFCs operate at high temperatures and use a molten carbonate electrolyte, ideal for large-scale power generation. DMFCs use a liquid methanol fuel and are commonly used in portable electronic devices.</p></p>
