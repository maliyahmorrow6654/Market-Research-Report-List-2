<p><p>The Indian whiskies market can be classified into three types: malt whisky, grain whiskey, and others. Malt whisky is made from malted barley, distilled using pot stills, and aged in oak casks. It is known for its rich flavors and complex aromas. Grain whiskey, on the other hand, is made from a mixture of grains like corn, wheat, and barley, distilled using continuous stills, and aged in oak casks. It tends to be smoother and lighter compared to malt whisky. The "others" category includes whiskies made from different grains or using different production methods, resulting in unique flavors and characteristics.</p></p>
