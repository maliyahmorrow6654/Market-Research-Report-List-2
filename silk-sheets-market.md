<p><p>Silk sheets are available in various market types, each derived from a different type of silk. Mulberry silk, the most common and luxurious type, is produced from silkworms fed on mulberry leaves. Tussah silk comes from wild silkworms and has a slightly coarser texture. Castor silk is produced by silkworms feeding on castor leaves and is known for its rich golden hue. Cassava silk is a plant-based silk made from the fibers of the cassava plant. These different market types offer consumers diverse choices when it comes to silk sheets.</p></p>
