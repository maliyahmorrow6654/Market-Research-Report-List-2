<p><p>Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) CO2 sensors are used to measure the concentration of carbon dioxide in the air. The amount of light absorbed is then measured, which helps determine the CO2 concentration.</p><p>The Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) CO2 sensors market is experiencing significant growth. <p><p>Non-dispersive Infrared (NDIR) CO2 Sensors are used to detect and measure carbon dioxide gas levels in various applications. The Single Channel NDIR CO2 Sensors can measure CO2 concentration in real-time using a single channel for both emission and absorption. On the other hand, Dual Channel NDIR CO2 Sensors use two separate channels - one for measuring the gas sample and the other for reference. This helps to eliminate any measurement errors and provide more accurate CO2 concentration readings. Both types of sensors are commonly used in industries, HVAC systems, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
